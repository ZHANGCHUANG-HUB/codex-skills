# Social Copy Rules

## Caption

Write one polished caption in Chinese unless the user requests another language. Make it feel like a real lifestyle note rather than campaign copy.

Suggested movement:

1. Open with a small moment, observation, or mood.
2. Add two or three concrete sensory or situational details.
3. Introduce the product naturally if present, focusing on how it fits the moment.
4. End with a restrained emotional afterthought or relatable line.

Keep the copy easy to scan. Short paragraphs are welcome. Avoid unsupported performance, health, or superiority claims.

## Titles

Provide exactly three distinct angles:

- Mood or emotion
- Scene or occasion
- Light discovery or recommendation

Keep titles conversational and specific. Avoid clickbait, exaggerated punctuation, and generic phrases that could fit any post.

## Cover line

Provide one short phrase that remains legible on a mobile cover. Prefer approximately 6–14 Chinese characters. It should create mood or context rather than sell aggressively.

## Hashtags

Provide exactly five relevant tags unless requested otherwise. Balance:

- One broad lifestyle category
- One concrete subject or product category
- One scene or occasion
- One aesthetic or photographic style
- One mood or audience-interest tag

Adapt tag language to the selected platform when the user specifies one.
