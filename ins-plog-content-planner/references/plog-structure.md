# PLOG Sequence Structures

## Six-image sequence

1. Opening atmosphere: establish time, place, palette, and emotional temperature.
2. Scene detail: show a tactile environmental fragment or supporting object.
3. Subject or product introduction: reveal the main subject clearly but naturally.
4. Interaction or use: show a believable hand action or lived-in moment.
5. Detail close-up: reinforce material, texture, label, food surface, or sensory cue.
6. Closing atmosphere: end with a quiet, memorable frame and visual breathing room.

## Nine-image sequence

1. Opening atmosphere.
2. Environmental detail.
3. First subject or product appearance.
4. Human or hand interaction.
5. Core hero image.
6. Detail close-up.
7. Deeper use scene.
8. Emotional closing moment.
9. Summary still life or final freeze frame.

## Product coverage

Across a product-led set, cover as many of these roles as the count permits:

- Lifestyle hero image
- Context or environment image
- Natural use or interaction image
- Packaging or label detail image
- Material, texture, or sensory detail image
- Product-as-supporting-character image
- Product-free atmosphere image when it improves authenticity

Avoid making every frame a centered hero shot. Keep the product clear enough to recognize in the frames where it matters, while allowing some frames to build the life around it.

## Rhythm and continuity

- Alternate wide, medium, and close views.
- Alternate static still life with one or two restrained interaction frames.
- Reuse a small prop family rather than adding unrelated props to every image.
- Preserve a coherent location, time-of-day family, color palette, and surface language.
- Let the final frame echo an object, color, or light motif from the opening frame.
